\abstract{In Vietnamese legal question answering, the appropriate retrieval
strategy varies with the query: simple questions require direct textual
lookup, relational questions demand reasoning over legal structures such as
amendment and cross-document reference, and ambiguous questions risk
grounding answers in the wrong legal target. We propose a Hybrid GraphRAG
system with a two-stage, reasoning-aware adaptive router that chooses among
dense retrieval, graph traversal, hybrid reasoning, and clarification before
any evidence is retrieved. A lightweight XGBoost first stage handles clear
cases at millisecond latency, a selective large language model (LLM) verifier
is invoked only for uncertain, ambiguous, or relation-heavy queries, and a
deterministic history resolver settles conversational references before
routing. The system operates over a dense chunk index and a Vietnamese legal
knowledge graph built from the official codification. On a strict
$600$-query benchmark with bootstrap
confidence intervals, the two-stage router reaches answer F1 of $0.640$,
statistically indistinguishable from the strongest fixed strategy
(always-dense, $0.663$), while achieving $71\%$ higher exact match and
intercepting every ambiguous query (clarify F1 of $0.972$) on a $234$-query
diagnostic. A controlled ablation that holds routing labels at gold values
shows the second stage causally contributes $+0.074$~F1 ($0.637$ vs
$0.563$), confirming that it acts as a chain-of-thought step rather than a
route corrector; selective invocation at a $79.5\%$ trigger rate matches
always-on verification while adding only $6\%$ latency over never
verifying. The first-stage classifier reaches
$0.898$ routing accuracy on the paraphrase-hardened strict split.}
